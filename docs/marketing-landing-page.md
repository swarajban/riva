---

**How does Riva access my calendar?**

You connect a Gmail account for Riva to use as your assistant. This account has access to Gmail (to send/receive emails) and Google Calendar (to check availability and create events). Your personal login is separate and only used to verify your identity.

---

**Does Riva send emails automatically?**

By default, Riva sends scheduling emails after a short delay (2-7 minutes) to feel natural. You can enable email approval mode to review every outbound email before it's sentâ€”useful when you're getting started or for high-stakes scheduling.

---

**What if I need to reschedule or cancel?**

Just reply to the text confirmation with your changes. Say "tomorrow" to find new times, "45" to change duration, or "N" to cancel. If the other party requests changes, Riva will text you for approval before taking action.

---

**How is my data protected?**

Riva only accesses the Gmail account you connect as your assistant. We don't store email content long-termâ€”it's processed to understand scheduling context and then discarded. Calendar data is accessed in real-time via Google's API, not stored.

---

**Can I use Riva with Outlook or other email providers?**

Currently, Riva only works with Gmail and Google Calendar. Outlook/Microsoft 365 support is on our roadmap.

---

**What's the cancellation policy?**

Cancel anytime from your account settings. You'll retain access through the end of your billing period. No long-term contracts, no cancellation fees.

---

**Is there a free trial?**

We don't offer a free trial, but you can cancel within the first 14 days for a full refund if Riva isn't right for you.
